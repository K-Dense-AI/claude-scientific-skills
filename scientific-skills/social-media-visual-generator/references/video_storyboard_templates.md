# Video Storyboard Templates

Ready-to-use storyboard structures for Reels, TikToks, Shorts, and other short-form video. Each template includes shot-by-shot breakdowns with timing, text overlays, transitions, and music direction.

---

## 1. 15-Second Reel: Hook - Content - CTA

**Best for:** Quick tips, single facts, product teasers, announcements.
**Platforms:** Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts.

### Shot List

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:03 | 3s | Close-up or text card | **HOOK:** Bold statement, question, or surprising visual that stops the scroll. | Hook text, large, centered. 2-5 words. | Hard cut in. |
| 2 | 0:03-0:07 | 4s | Medium shot or B-roll | **CONTENT:** Deliver the core message, show the product, or explain the point. | Key phrase or keyword, lower third. | Cut or zoom transition. |
| 3 | 0:07-0:11 | 4s | Close-up or demo | **PROOF/DETAIL:** Supporting visual, quick demo, or result. | Supporting text or stat, centered. | Smooth cut. |
| 4 | 0:11-0:15 | 4s | Medium shot or text card | **CTA:** Direct instruction -- follow, save, link in bio, comment. | CTA text, large. Handle/username. | Fade or brand end card. |

### Music Direction
- Upbeat, trending audio. Beat drop or hook at 0:00.
- Energy peak at 0:07-0:11 during proof/detail.
- Clean ending on beat at 0:15.

### Key Principles
- First frame must be visually arresting (no blank screens, no slow intros).
- One idea only. If it needs explanation, use 30s template instead.
- Text should be readable in under 2 seconds per overlay.

## 2. 30-Second Reel: Hook - Problem - Solution - CTA

**Best for:** Tips with context, mini-tutorials, product benefits, myth-busting.
**Platforms:** Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts.

### Shot List

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:03 | 3s | Direct to camera or text card | **HOOK:** Provocative question or bold claim. "Stop doing X" or "Did you know...?" | Hook text, large, bold. | Hard cut in. |
| 2 | 0:03-0:08 | 5s | B-roll or demonstration | **PROBLEM:** Show or explain the pain point. Relatable scenario. | Problem statement text, lower third. | Cut. |
| 3 | 0:08-0:12 | 4s | Direct to camera or product shot | **PIVOT:** "Here's the fix" or "Instead, try this." Bridge from problem to solution. | Pivot phrase, centered. | Whip pan or quick zoom. |
| 4 | 0:12-0:20 | 8s | Demo, walkthrough, or results | **SOLUTION:** Show the solution in action. Step-by-step if needed (2-3 quick steps). | Step labels or key benefit text. | Cuts between steps. |
| 5 | 0:20-0:25 | 5s | Results shot or before/after | **PROOF:** Show the outcome, results, or transformation. | Result stat or "before vs after" label. | Side-by-side or morph. |
| 6 | 0:25-0:30 | 5s | Direct to camera or end card | **CTA:** "Follow for more," "Save this," "Link in bio," or question to drive comments. | CTA + handle. Brand colors. | Fade to brand card. |

### Music Direction
- Trending sound or original audio. Conversational tone if voiceover.
- Build energy from 0:00 to 0:20, peak at solution reveal.
- Softer or resolved feel at CTA.

### Key Principles
- Problem must be instantly recognizable to the target audience.
- Solution reveal should feel satisfying (visual or audio cue at the pivot).
- Keep solution explanation to 3 steps maximum.

## 3. 60-Second TikTok: Hook - Story - Twist - CTA

**Best for:** Storytelling, in-depth tutorials, day-in-the-life, educational deep dives.
**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.

### Shot List

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:03 | 3s | Close-up or POV | **HOOK:** Pattern interrupt. Unexpected visual, bold claim, or mid-action start (in medias res). | Hook text, large. | Hard cut in. No fade. |
| 2 | 0:03-0:08 | 5s | Medium shot or B-roll | **CONTEXT:** Set the scene. Who, what, where. Establish the situation. | Context label if needed. | Smooth cut. |
| 3 | 0:08-0:18 | 10s | Mixed shots | **RISING ACTION:** Build the story. Show the process, the journey, or the challenge unfolding. Multiple quick cuts (2-3s each). | Key phrases at each cut. | Quick cuts, 2-3s each. |
| 4 | 0:18-0:25 | 7s | Close-up or reaction | **COMPLICATION:** Introduce the obstacle, the unexpected, or the "but then..." moment. | Tension text or "But..." label. | Dramatic pause or slow-mo. |
| 5 | 0:25-0:35 | 10s | Demo or walkthrough | **RESOLUTION:** The breakthrough, the solution, or the reveal. Slower pacing for emphasis. | Resolution text, step labels if tutorial. | Reveal transition (zoom out, uncover). |
| 6 | 0:35-0:45 | 10s | Results or montage | **PAYOFF:** Show the final result, the transformation, or the "after." Montage of outcomes if multiple. | Result stats, "Final result" label. | Montage cuts or split screen. |
| 7 | 0:45-0:52 | 7s | Direct to camera | **REFLECTION:** Key takeaway. What did we learn? Why does this matter? Personal connection. | Takeaway text, clean. | Soft cut. |
| 8 | 0:52-0:60 | 8s | End card or direct to camera | **CTA:** Engagement driver. "What would you do?" "Follow for part 2." "Comment your answer." | CTA + handle + follow prompt. | Brand end card or loop point. |

### Music Direction
- Story-driven audio: builds tension at 0:18-0:25, releases at 0:25-0:35.
- If voiceover: conversational, paced for clarity. Pause at complication.
- Music volume dips under voiceover, rises during montage.

### Key Principles
- In medias res opening (start in the middle of the action).
- Complication at ~30% mark creates retention hook.
- Loop-worthy: last frame connects visually or thematically to first frame.

## 4. 90-Second Educational Template

**Best for:** Explainers, deep tutorials, myth-busting series, educational content.
**Platforms:** TikTok (up to 3 min), Instagram Reels (up to 90s).

### Shot List

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:05 | 5s | Direct to camera or motion graphic | **HOOK:** "Most people get this wrong about [topic]" or attention-grabbing claim. | Hook text, bold and large. | Hard cut. |
| 2 | 0:05-0:12 | 7s | B-roll or diagram | **FOUNDATION:** Establish baseline knowledge. What the audience thinks they know. | Definition or common belief text. | Smooth cut. |
| 3 | 0:12-0:20 | 8s | Direct to camera or animation | **MISCONCEPTION:** "But here's what actually happens..." Reveal the gap in understanding. | "Myth:" or "What most people think:" label. | Visual "break" effect (glitch, crack, shatter). |
| 4 | 0:20-0:35 | 15s | Mixed: diagrams, demos, B-roll | **EXPLANATION:** Core educational content. Break into 2-3 sub-points with visual support. | Numbered points or keyword labels. | Cut between sub-points. |
| 5 | 0:35-0:50 | 15s | Walkthrough or demo | **EXAMPLE:** Concrete example or case study that illustrates the explanation. | Example label. Key data highlighted. | Smooth cuts within example. |
| 6 | 0:50-0:60 | 10s | Direct to camera or infographic | **APPLICATION:** "So what does this mean for you?" Practical implication. | "What this means:" header. Action items listed. | Cut. |
| 7 | 0:60-0:75 | 15s | Quick-cut montage or demo | **ADVANCED TIP:** Bonus insight for those still watching. Rewards retention. | "Bonus:" or "Pro tip:" label. | Energetic cut or zoom. |
| 8 | 0:75-0:90 | 15s | Direct to camera + end card | **SUMMARY + CTA:** Recap the key point in one sentence. CTA to follow, save, or check link. | Summary sentence. CTA. Handle. | Fade to brand end card. |

### Music Direction
- Calm, focused background music. Think "documentary" or "explainer" tone.
- Volume low enough for clear voiceover.
- Subtle energy lift at advanced tip section (0:60-0:75).
- Clean fade at end.

### Key Principles
- Authority positioning: demonstrate expertise through specificity and examples.
- Retention strategy: misconception reveal at 0:12 and bonus at 0:60 create two retention hooks.
- Visual variety: change shot type every 7-10 seconds to maintain attention.

## 5. Behind-the-Scenes Template

**Best for:** Process reveals, day-in-the-life, brand authenticity, creative process.
**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.
**Duration:** 30-60 seconds.

### Shot List

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:03 | 3s | Finished product or result | **HOOK:** Show the polished end result first. Create curiosity about the process. | "How we made this" or "The process behind [X]." | Hard cut. |
| 2 | 0:03-0:08 | 5s | Wide shot of workspace | **SETTING:** Establish the environment. Workshop, office, kitchen, studio. | Location/context label. | Cut. |
| 3 | 0:08-0:18 | 10s | Close-up hands/tools + medium shots | **EARLY PROCESS:** Raw materials, first steps, messy beginnings. Multiple quick angles. | Step or material labels. | Quick cuts, 2-3s each. |
| 4 | 0:18-0:28 | 10s | Time-lapse or quick-cut montage | **MID PROCESS:** The bulk of the work. Compressed into satisfying sequence. | Progress indicators if relevant. | Time-lapse or montage. |
| 5 | 0:28-0:38 | 10s | Close-up detail work | **REFINEMENT:** The finishing touches, quality checks, fine details. | Detail callouts. | Slow-mo for precision moments. |
| 6 | 0:38-0:45 | 7s | Reveal shot | **FINAL REVEAL:** The completed work in its full glory. Satisfying reveal moment. | Product/project name. | Dramatic reveal (uncover, zoom out, rotate). |
| 7 | 0:45-0:50 | 5s | Direct to camera or end card | **CTA:** "Want to see more? Follow." or "Which should we make next?" | CTA + handle. | Fade to brand card. |

### Music Direction
- Trending "making of" or satisfying process audio.
- ASMR-adjacent if process has satisfying sounds (use natural audio + music).
- Build energy through the process, peak at reveal.

## 6. Product Showcase Template

**Best for:** Product launches, feature demos, unboxing, comparison.
**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.
**Duration:** 15-30 seconds.

### Shot List (30s version)

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:03 | 3s | Product hero shot | **HOOK:** Product in motion or dramatic lighting. No context yet -- pure visual appeal. | Product name or none. | Hard cut or unbox moment. |
| 2 | 0:03-0:08 | 5s | Close-up details | **DETAIL:** Texture, material, key design element close-ups. | Feature callouts (material, size, etc.). | Quick cuts between angles. |
| 3 | 0:08-0:15 | 7s | In-use or lifestyle | **CONTEXT:** Product being used in real life. Solve the problem it was designed for. | Benefit statement, lower third. | Smooth cut. |
| 4 | 0:15-0:22 | 7s | Feature comparison or demo | **DIFFERENTIATOR:** What makes it unique. Side-by-side, or key feature demo. | "Unlike others..." or feature highlight. | Split screen or zoom. |
| 5 | 0:22-0:27 | 5s | Satisfied user or result | **PROOF:** Happy usage moment, result, or social proof. | Rating, review quote, or result stat. | Cut. |
| 6 | 0:27-0:30 | 3s | Product + brand end card | **CTA:** Where to buy, discount code, link in bio. | Price (optional), CTA, brand. | Brand card. |

### Music Direction
- Upbeat, premium feel. Trending audio preferred.
- Beat synced to product reveal moments.
- Clean, modern production.

## 7. Testimonial Template

**Best for:** Customer stories, reviews, case study results, social proof.
**Platforms:** Instagram Reels, TikTok, LinkedIn Video.
**Duration:** 30-60 seconds.

### Shot List (45s version)

| # | Time | Duration | Shot Type | Description | Text Overlay | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:04 | 4s | Text card or pull quote | **HOOK:** Most compelling quote from the testimonial. The result or emotion. | Quote text, large, with quotation marks. | Fade in or hard cut. |
| 2 | 0:04-0:10 | 6s | Talking head or voiceover + B-roll | **BEFORE:** "Before I found [product/service], I was struggling with..." | Name, title, lower third. "Before" label. | Cut. |
| 3 | 0:10-0:18 | 8s | Talking head or re-enactment | **PROBLEM:** Specific pain points. Relatable frustration. Concrete examples. | Pain point keywords appearing on screen. | Cut between angles. |
| 4 | 0:18-0:25 | 7s | Product shot + talking head | **DISCOVERY:** "Then I found [solution]." How they discovered it. | Product/service name appearance. | Transition to brighter tone. |
| 5 | 0:25-0:35 | 10s | Results footage or B-roll | **RESULTS:** Specific outcomes. Numbers, transformations, quality of life improvements. | Stats and results on screen. Before/after if applicable. | Dynamic cuts. |
| 6 | 0:35-0:40 | 5s | Talking head close-up | **EMOTIONAL PAYOFF:** How they feel now. The human impact. Genuine emotion. | Key emotional quote highlighted. | Soft cut. |
| 7 | 0:40-0:45 | 5s | Product/brand + CTA | **CTA:** "Try it yourself." Offer, link, or next step. | CTA + offer code if applicable. Brand logo. | Brand end card. |

### Music Direction
- Warm, emotional undertone. Not overpowering -- voice is the star.
- Uplift at results section (0:25-0:35).
- Gentle resolution at emotional payoff.

## Universal Video Guidelines

### Retention Optimization
- **0-3 seconds:** 50% of viewers decide to stay or leave. Front-load the hook.
- **Pattern interrupts** every 7-10 seconds: change shot type, angle, or add text.
- **Open loops:** Hint at what's coming ("Wait for the end...") to reduce early drop-off.
- **Loop point:** Design the last frame to visually connect to the first for auto-replay retention.

### Text Overlay Best Practices
- Maximum 5-7 words per text overlay.
- Display each overlay for minimum 2 seconds.
- Position text in the center 80% of the frame (avoid edges cropped by platforms).
- Use brand fonts with high contrast (white text with black stroke on any background).
- Animate text entrances (pop, slide, typewriter) for engagement.

### Safe Zones for All Vertical Video (1080x1920)
- **Top 250px:** Platform UI (username, follow button, music info). Avoid text.
- **Bottom 300px:** Caption area, CTA buttons, navigation. Avoid small text.
- **Right 120px (bottom half):** Like, comment, share, sound icons. Avoid content.
- **Safe content area:** 810 x 1370 px centered (135px from sides, 250px from top, 300px from bottom).

### Transition Types Reference
| Transition | Feel | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Hard cut | Energetic, fast-paced | Hooks, quick tips, montages |
| Smooth cut | Professional, seamless | Tutorials, educational content |
| Whip pan | Dynamic, exciting | Reveals, pivots, location changes |
| Zoom in/out | Focus shift, reveal | Detail shots, context setting |
| Morph/warp | Magical, transformative | Before/after, transformations |
| Fade | Gentle, conclusive | Endings, reflective moments |
| Match cut | Creative, satisfying | Object transitions, visual storytelling |
| Split screen | Comparative | Before/after, vs. content, duets |
| Slow-mo | Dramatic, detailed | Precision work, emotional peaks |
| Time-lapse | Compressed time | Process, behind-the-scenes |

### Audio Guidelines
- **Voiceover:** Record in a quiet space. Use lapel mic or phone close to mouth.
- **Music:** Use royalty-free or platform-provided sounds to avoid takedowns.
- **Trending audio:** Boosts discoverability but may not fit brand. Use strategically.
- **Original audio:** Best for authority positioning and brand recognition.
- **Mix levels:** Voice at -6dB, music at -18dB to -24dB under voice.
