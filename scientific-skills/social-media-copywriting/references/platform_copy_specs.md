# Platform Copy Specifications

Comprehensive reference for character limits, formatting capabilities, and content best practices across all major social media platforms.

---

## Instagram

### Character Limits
| Element         | Limit              | Notes                                    |
|-----------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Caption         | 2,200 characters   | Includes spaces and emoji                |
| Bio             | 150 characters     | Single link allowed                      |
| Username        | 30 characters      | Letters, numbers, periods, underscores   |
| Comments        | 2,200 characters   | Same as caption                          |
| Hashtags        | 30 per post        | In caption or first comment              |
| Alt text        | 100 characters     | For accessibility                        |
| Reel caption    | 2,200 characters   | Shorter displays better on mobile        |
| Story text      | ~200 characters    | Depends on font size and placement       |
| DM              | 1,000 characters   | Per message                              |

### Truncation / "More" Cutoff
- **Feed posts:** First ~125 characters are visible before "more" on mobile
- **Reels:** First ~55-80 characters visible in feed, ~125 on reel page
- **Rule:** Your hook MUST live in the first 125 characters

### Optimal Caption Length
- **Engagement posts:** 150-300 characters (quick read, high engagement)
- **Storytelling posts:** 800-1,500 characters (deeper connection)
- **Educational carousels:** 100-200 characters (let slides do the talking)
- **Reels:** 50-150 characters (video carries the message)

### Hashtag Best Practices
- Use 5-15 hashtags (sweet spot for reach without looking spammy)
- Mix sizes: 30% small (<50K posts), 40% medium (50K-500K), 30% large (500K+)
- Place in first comment or after 5 line breaks in caption
- Include 1-2 branded hashtags consistently
- Rotate hashtag sets to avoid shadowban triggers
- Research hashtags monthly; remove underperformers

### Formatting Capabilities
- No bold, italic, or underline natively (use Unicode generators for bio)
- Line breaks work (press Enter twice in app, use invisible character trick)
- Bullet points via emoji (use consistent emoji bullets)
- No clickable links in captions (bio link or story link sticker only)
- Mentions (@) and hashtags (#) are clickable

### Tone Expectations
- Visual-first: caption supports the image/video
- Personal and relatable
- Aspirational but authentic
- Storytelling and vulnerability perform well
- Avoid corporate jargon

### Best Performing Content Types
1. Carousel posts (highest saves and shares)
2. Reels (highest reach)
3. Story polls/questions (highest engagement rate)
4. User-generated content reposts
5. Behind-the-scenes content
6. Before/after transformations

## LinkedIn

### Character Limits
| Element         | Limit              | Notes                                    |
|-----------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Post            | 3,000 characters   | Standard posts                           |
| Article         | 120,000 characters | Long-form content                        |
| Headline        | 220 characters     | Profile headline                         |
| Summary/About   | 2,600 characters   | Profile about section                    |
| Comments        | 1,250 characters   | On posts                                 |
| Company page    | 2,000 characters   | About section                            |
| Ad headline     | 70 characters      | Sponsored content                        |
| Ad description  | 100 characters     | Sponsored content                        |

### Truncation / "See More" Cutoff
- **Desktop:** ~140 characters before "see more"
- **Mobile:** ~100 characters before "see more"
- **Rule:** Lead with a provocative hook in the first 100 characters

### Optimal Caption Length
- **Engagement posts:** 150-300 characters (quick insights)
- **Thought leadership:** 800-1,500 characters (detailed takes)
- **Story posts:** 1,500-2,500 characters (personal narratives)
- **Document/carousel intros:** 100-200 characters

### Hashtag Best Practices
- Use 3-5 hashtags maximum
- Place at the very end of the post
- Use industry-standard hashtags (#Leadership, #Marketing, #SaaS)
- Include 1 branded hashtag if applicable
- Avoid trending pop-culture hashtags (looks unprofessional)

### Formatting Capabilities
- Line breaks work (use them liberally for readability)
- No native bold or italic (use Unicode for emphasis sparingly)
- Bullet points via emoji or Unicode bullets
- Links are clickable in posts (but may reduce reach)
- Mentions (@) work for people and companies
- Document/PDF uploads display as carousels

### Tone Expectations
- Professional but personable
- Thought-provoking and contrarian opinions perform well
- Data and insights over fluff
- First-person narratives about professional experiences
- Avoid clickbait; the audience is sophisticated
- Vulnerability about professional challenges resonates

### Best Performing Content Types
1. Personal stories with professional lessons
2. Contrarian industry takes
3. Document carousels (how-to guides)
4. Polls (high engagement)
5. Celebration/milestone posts
6. Data-driven insights with original analysis

## TikTok

### Character Limits
| Element         | Limit              | Notes                                    |
|-----------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Caption         | 4,000 characters   | Expanded from previous 150               |
| Bio             | 80 characters      | Plus 1 link (1000+ followers)            |
| Username        | 24 characters      | Letters, numbers, underscores, periods   |
| Comments        | 150 characters     | Per comment                              |
| Video title     | 90 characters      | For search optimization                  |

### Truncation / Visibility
- **Feed:** First ~55 characters visible without tapping
- **For You page:** First 1-2 lines visible (roughly 80 characters)
- **Rule:** Hook must be in the first 55 characters

### Optimal Caption Length
- **Standard videos:** 50-150 characters (let the video talk)
- **Educational content:** 150-300 characters (add context)
- **SEO-optimized:** 300-500 characters (keyword-rich for search)
- **Storytelling:** Keep under 300 (audience scrolls fast)

### Hashtag Best Practices
- Use 3-5 hashtags
- Mix: 1 trending + 2-3 niche + 1 branded
- #FYP and #ForYou are debated (may not help, but rarely hurt)
- Place in caption, not comments
- Use hashtags as keywords for TikTok search

### Formatting Capabilities
- No bold, italic, or text formatting in captions
- Line breaks work
- No clickable links in captions (bio link only)
- Mentions (@) are clickable
- Hashtags (#) are clickable and act as search terms

### Tone Expectations
- Authentic and unpolished
- Conversational, like talking to a friend
- Trend-aware and culturally current
- Self-deprecating humor works well
- Avoid corporate-speak entirely
- Educational content framed as casual advice

### Best Performing Content Types
1. Hook-driven tutorials ("Stop doing X, do Y instead")
2. Trending audio with original content
3. Story-time content
4. Day-in-the-life
5. Before/after reveals
6. Duets and stitches with commentary

## Facebook

### Character Limits
| Element         | Limit              | Notes                                    |
|-----------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Post            | 63,206 characters  | But shorter performs far better           |
| Link post text  | 63,206 characters  | Text above the link preview              |
| Comments        | 8,000 characters   | Per comment                              |
| Page About      | 255 characters     | Short description                        |
| Page bio        | 101 characters     | Under page name                          |
| Ad headline     | 40 characters      | Recommended                              |
| Ad primary text | 125 characters     | Recommended (up to 2,200 allowed)        |
| Event name      | 64 characters      | Event title                              |
| Group name      | 100 characters     | Group title                              |

### Truncation / "See More" Cutoff
- **Desktop:** ~477 characters before "See More"
- **Mobile:** ~110 characters before "See More"
- **Link posts:** Only ~80-100 characters show above the link preview
- **Rule:** Front-load the value in the first 100 characters

### Optimal Caption Length
- **Link shares:** 40-80 characters (let the link preview work)
- **Photo posts:** 80-250 characters
- **Status updates:** 100-250 characters
- **Story posts:** 300-500 characters (narrative style)
- **Video posts:** 60-120 characters (video carries the message)

### Hashtag Best Practices
- Use 1-3 hashtags maximum (less is more on Facebook)
- Hashtags matter less here than any other platform
- Branded hashtags for campaigns only
- Do not copy Instagram hashtag blocks to Facebook

### Formatting Capabilities
- Bold and italic supported in some contexts (groups, comments)
- Line breaks work well
- Links are clickable and generate previews
- Mentions (@) for people and pages
- Lists and bullet points via emoji or dashes
- Poll creation built-in
- Events and fundraiser integrations

### Tone Expectations
- Conversational and community-oriented
- More relaxed than LinkedIn, more mature than TikTok
- Questions and opinions drive engagement
- Personal stories and relatable content
- Local community angle performs well
- Nostalgia and shared experiences resonate

### Best Performing Content Types
1. Native video (especially live video)
2. Posts that ask questions or opinions
3. Relatable memes and humor
4. Community polls
5. Event promotions
6. Group discussions and community content

## Twitter / X

### Character Limits
| Element         | Limit              | Notes                                    |
|-----------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Tweet           | 280 characters     | Standard accounts                        |
| Tweet (Premium) | 4,000 characters   | X Premium subscribers                    |
| Bio             | 160 characters     | Profile bio                              |
| Display name    | 50 characters      | Can include emoji                        |
| Username        | 15 characters      | Letters, numbers, underscores only       |
| DM              | 10,000 characters  | Per message                              |
| List name       | 25 characters      | List title                               |
| Alt text        | 1,000 characters   | Image descriptions                       |

### Truncation / Visibility
- **Standard tweets:** Full 280 visible (no truncation)
- **Long tweets (Premium):** First ~280 characters, then "Show more"
- **Quote tweets:** Original tweet takes visual space
- **Threads:** Each tweet stands alone visually

### Optimal Caption Length
- **Highest engagement:** 71-100 characters
- **Good range:** 100-200 characters
- **Threads:** 200-280 per tweet, 3-10 tweets per thread
- **Quote tweets:** 80-150 characters of commentary

### Hashtag Best Practices
- Use 1-2 hashtags maximum
- Integrate into the sentence when possible
- Trending hashtags only if genuinely relevant
- Branded hashtags for campaigns
- More than 2 hashtags reduces engagement

### Formatting Capabilities
- No bold, italic, or text formatting natively
- No line breaks within a tweet (use periods on new lines as workaround)
- Links are clickable and show previews (may reduce impressions)
- Mentions (@) are clickable
- Thread format for long-form content
- Polls built-in (up to 4 options)
- Spaces (audio) for live conversation

### Tone Expectations
- Witty and concise
- Opinionated and bold
- Conversational, like talking at a party
- Hot takes and contrarian views perform well
- Humor and cultural references land well
- Real-time commentary and newsjacking

### Best Performing Content Types
1. Hot takes and strong opinions
2. Threads (educational or storytelling)
3. Memes and humor
4. Quote tweets with smart commentary
5. Polls
6. Real-time event commentary
7. Screenshots from other platforms (with commentary)
