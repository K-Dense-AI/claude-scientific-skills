# Emotional Triggers & Copywriting Frameworks

Psychology-backed frameworks and power words for writing social media copy that drives action.

## Copywriting Frameworks for Social Media

### AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)

The classic framework adapted for social media. Works best for product launches, promotional posts, and conversion-focused content.

**Structure:**
1. **Attention:** Stop the scroll with a bold hook
2. **Interest:** Deliver a benefit or surprising fact
3. **Desire:** Create emotional connection or social proof
4. **Action:** Clear, single CTA

**Instagram Example:**
```
Your morning routine is costing you 2 hours a week.          [ATTENTION]

We tracked 500 professionals and found that the average      [INTEREST]
morning prep takes 47 minutes. The top performers?
Just 19 minutes.

Our clients cut their morning routine in half and report      [DESIRE]
feeling MORE put-together, not less.

Grab the free Morning Reset Guide (link in bio)              [ACTION]
```

**LinkedIn Example:**
```
I lost my biggest client last Tuesday.                        [ATTENTION]

But here's what I learned from the exit interview             [INTEREST]
that every service provider needs to hear:

They didn't leave because of price.                           [DESIRE]
They left because we stopped surprising them.
Since then, we've implemented a "delight protocol"
and retention is up 34%.

What's your client retention strategy?                        [ACTION]
Share in the comments.
```

**TikTok Example:**
```
POV: You stopped doing this one thing and your skin cleared   [ATTENTION]
up in 2 weeks.                                                [INTEREST]

(video shows the routine change)                              [DESIRE]

Follow for more skincare science.                             [ACTION]
```

### PAS (Problem - Agitation - Solution)

The most powerful framework for pain-point marketing. Works best for educational content, service promotion, and health/wellness niches.

**Structure:**
1. **Problem:** Name the specific pain your audience feels
2. **Agitation:** Twist the knife; show what happens if they don't solve it
3. **Solution:** Present your offer as the natural resolution

**Instagram Example:**
```
Spending 3 hours on a caption only to get 12 likes?          [PROBLEM]

Meanwhile, your competitor posts a blurry photo with         [AGITATION]
a one-liner and gets 500 engagements. The algorithm
doesn't care about effort. It cares about strategy.

That's why we built the Caption Formula Kit.                  [SOLUTION]
Templates, hooks, and hashtag sets that actually work.
Link in bio to grab yours.
```

**LinkedIn Example:**
```
Your best employees are quietly applying elsewhere.           [PROBLEM]

They won't tell you. They'll smile in 1:1s, hit their        [AGITATION]
targets, and one day hand in their notice. By then,
replacing them costs 6-9 months of their salary.

Here are 5 signals I've learned to watch for after            [SOLUTION]
losing 3 A-players in one quarter.

(Thread below)
```

### BAB (Before - After - Bridge)

Perfect for transformation stories, testimonials, and aspirational content.

**Structure:**
1. **Before:** Describe the painful current state
2. **After:** Paint the desirable future state
3. **Bridge:** Show how your product/service connects the two

**Instagram Example:**
```
BEFORE: Setting 5 alarms, snoozing all of them,              [BEFORE]
rushing out the door with mismatched socks.

AFTER: Waking up at 5:30 AM naturally, journaling            [AFTER]
with coffee, arriving early with a clear head.

THE BRIDGE: It wasn't discipline. It was redesigning          [BRIDGE]
my evening routine. Here are the 4 changes I made
(save this post).
```

**Facebook Example:**
```
6 months ago, our community group had 47 members             [BEFORE]
and zero engagement.

Today: 12,000 members, daily discussions, and members        [AFTER]
meeting up in real life.

What changed? We stopped posting content AT people           [BRIDGE]
and started creating conversations WITH them.
Here's exactly how we did it...
```

### 4U Framework (Useful - Urgent - Unique - Ultra-specific)

Best for short-form content, Twitter/X, and headlines. Every piece of copy should score high on at least 3 of the 4 Us.

**Components:**
- **Useful:** Does it solve a real problem?
- **Urgent:** Is there a reason to act now?
- **Unique:** Is this angle fresh?
- **Ultra-specific:** Are you concrete, not vague?

**Twitter/X Examples:**
```
[All 4U] The exact cold email template that booked me
14 meetings last week (stealing it costs you nothing,
not stealing it costs you pipeline). Thread:

[3U - no urgency] 73% of LinkedIn posts fail because
of the first line. Here's the hook formula top creators
use (with 5 real examples):

[4U] Last 48 hours to grab our SEO checklist before
we pull it down. 2,300 people already downloaded it.
Free, 1-page PDF, no email required.
```

### SPIN Framework (Situation - Problem - Implication - Need-payoff)

Adapted from sales methodology. Ideal for LinkedIn thought leadership and B2B content.

**Structure:**
1. **Situation:** Set the scene with a relatable context
2. **Problem:** Identify the specific challenge
3. **Implication:** Show the downstream consequences
4. **Need-payoff:** Reveal the value of solving it

**LinkedIn Example:**
```
Most B2B companies have a content calendar.                   [SITUATION]

But 80% of that content gets less than 50 views.             [PROBLEM]

That means your team is spending 20+ hours/week              [IMPLICATION]
creating content that doesn't generate pipeline.
At an average content marketer salary, that's
$45K/year in wasted effort.

Companies that switched to a demand-gen-first                 [NEED-PAYOFF]
content strategy saw 3x pipeline contribution
in 6 months.

Here's the framework we use with our clients:
```

## Cialdini's 6 Principles of Persuasion (Social Media Adaptations)

### 1. Reciprocity
**Principle:** People feel obligated to return favors.
**Social media application:** Give massive value for free.

**Tactics:**
- Share your best frameworks, templates, and tools for free
- Create "save-worthy" content (checklists, guides, cheat sheets)
- Offer free audits, reviews, or consultations
- Reply thoughtfully to every comment

**Example:**
```
I spent 6 months building this content calendar template.
Normally I'd charge for it, but I want to help this community.
Free download in comments. No email required.
```

### 2. Commitment & Consistency
**Principle:** Once people commit to something, they want to stay consistent.
**Social media application:** Get micro-commitments first.

**Tactics:**
- Ask for small engagements first (polls, emoji reactions)
- Build challenge series ("Join the 7-day challenge")
- Use "Type YES if you agree" prompts
- Create identity-based content ("You're the kind of person who...")

**Example:**
```
If you believe learning should never stop, drop a book emoji below.
(Tomorrow I'm sharing the 5 books that changed my career.
You won't want to miss it.)
```

### 3. Social Proof
**Principle:** People follow the actions of others.
**Social media application:** Show that others trust and use your product.

**Tactics:**
- Share metrics ("10,000+ customers", "500+ five-star reviews")
- Repost user-generated content and testimonials
- Highlight community size and engagement
- Show real results with real names (with permission)
- Use "Join X others who..." framing

**Example:**
```
3,247 founders downloaded our pitch deck template last month.
Here's what one of them said:
"Used it for my Series A. Raised $4M."
Grab yours - link in bio.
```

### 4. Authority
**Principle:** People follow credible experts.
**Social media application:** Establish and demonstrate expertise.

**Tactics:**
- Lead with credentials when relevant ("After 10 years in...")
- Share data and original research
- Reference reputable sources
- Show behind-the-scenes of your process
- Get featured in or reference major publications

**Example:**
```
After auditing 200+ landing pages this year, here are the
3 mistakes I see on almost every single one.
(Coming from someone who's been in CRO for 12 years.)
```

### 5. Liking
**Principle:** People say yes to people they like.
**Social media application:** Be relatable, authentic, and human.

**Tactics:**
- Share personal stories and vulnerabilities
- Use humor and self-deprecation
- Show your face (personal brand > logo posts)
- Compliment your audience genuinely
- Find common ground with your audience

**Example:**
```
Not going to lie, I almost didn't post this.
But the messy, unfiltered version of entrepreneurship
is the one nobody talks about. So here goes...
```

### 6. Scarcity
**Principle:** People want what they can't easily have.
**Social media application:** Create legitimate urgency and exclusivity.

**Tactics:**
- Limited-time offers with real deadlines
- Limited spots ("Only 10 seats left")
- Early-access or waitlist models
- Exclusive content for engaged followers
- Seasonal or one-time offers

**Example:**
```
We only open enrollment twice a year.
Fall cohort starts October 1.
23 of 30 spots are filled.
DM "READY" for the application link.
```

## Emotional Trigger Combinations

The most powerful copy combines multiple triggers. Here are proven combinations:

### Curiosity + Urgency
```
The pricing hack nobody talks about (and we're pulling this post Friday).
```

### Social Proof + Scarcity
```
12,000 people downloaded this template. Only available until midnight.
```

### Authority + Vulnerability
```
After 15 years in marketing, here's the embarrassing mistake
I still make (and how I finally fixed it).
```

### Exclusivity + Reciprocity
```
I normally charge $500 for this audit. But for my followers,
I'm doing 10 for free this month. DM "AUDIT" to claim yours.
```

### FOMO + Social Proof
```
47 founders in our community launched this quarter.
Applications for the next cohort close in 3 days.
```

## Hook Formulas

High-performing first lines follow these patterns:

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Number + Unexpected claim | "I made $10K from a post that took 4 minutes to write." |
| "Stop doing X" | "Stop posting motivational quotes. Here's what to do instead." |
| Question that challenges | "What if everything you know about hashtags is wrong?" |
| Bold declaration | "Cold email is not dead. You're just bad at it." |
| "I [vulnerable admission]" | "I got fired from my dream job. Best thing that ever happened." |
| "The [number] mistake" | "The #1 mistake new founders make on LinkedIn." |
| "Here's why" | "Here's why your reels get 200 views (and how to fix it)." |
| "POV:" | "POV: You finally stopped chasing followers and started building community." |
| "Unpopular opinion:" | "Unpopular opinion: You don't need a content calendar." |
| "[Timeframe] ago" | "6 months ago I had 200 followers. Today I have 50K. Here's what changed." |
