# Competitor Analysis Guide

Methodology for analyzing competitors in the context of social media and digital brand presence. Produces structured data for the `competitors` section of `brand_dna.json` and informs content strategy.

## 1. Competitor Selection

### Direct Competitors

Brands that offer similar products or services to the same target market. Select 3-5 for meaningful analysis without dilution.

**Selection criteria:**

- Same industry and market segment
- Similar price point or tier
- Overlapping target audience
- Active social media presence (minimum 2 platforms)
- Comparable company size (within 1 order of magnitude in revenue or team size)

### Aspirational Competitors

1-2 brands that the target brand aspires to emulate, even if they are in a different size category or adjacent industry. Useful for identifying best practices and setting long-term benchmarks.

### Category Disruptors

1-2 newer or unconventional players that are changing how the industry communicates on social media. Useful for spotting emerging trends.

## 2. SWOT Analysis for Social Media

For each competitor, evaluate four dimensions specifically through a social media lens.

### Strengths

What the competitor does well on social media:

- **Content quality**: Production value, copywriting, visual consistency
- **Engagement rate**: Likes, comments, shares relative to follower count
- **Community building**: Active comment responses, user-generated content programs
- **Platform mastery**: Effective use of platform-specific features (Reels, Stories, LinkedIn articles)
- **Consistency**: Regular posting cadence, visual coherence across posts
- **Influencer partnerships**: Quality and relevance of collaborations
- **Viral content**: Posts that significantly outperform their baseline

#### Evaluation Questions

- What posts get the highest engagement? What pattern do they share?
- How quickly and thoughtfully does the brand respond to comments?
- Is their visual feed cohesive when viewed as a grid?
- Do they use platform-native features effectively?

### Weaknesses

Where the competitor falls short:

- **Neglected platforms**: Platforms where they have presence but low activity
- **Low engagement**: High follower count but minimal interaction
- **Inconsistency**: Irregular posting, shifting visual styles, tone changes
- **Negative sentiment**: Complaints in comments, PR issues, unaddressed criticism
- **Stale content**: Repetitive formats, no experimentation, dated design
- **Poor responsiveness**: Slow or absent replies to audience questions
- **Over-promotion**: Too much sales content, not enough value

#### Evaluation Questions

- Which platforms do they seem to neglect or post infrequently on?
- Are there recurring complaints in their comments or reviews?
- Does their content feel repetitive or formulaic?
- What is the ratio of promotional to value-adding content?

### Opportunities

Gaps in the competitor's strategy that the target brand can exploit:

- **Underserved topics**: Content themes the competitor ignores
- **Format gaps**: Content types they never use (e.g., no video, no carousels)
- **Platform gaps**: Platforms where the competitor is absent but the audience is active
- **Community needs**: Questions from the audience that go unanswered
- **Trend gaps**: Trending formats or topics the competitor has not adopted
- **Audience segments**: Sub-audiences the competitor does not target directly

#### Evaluation Questions

- What questions does their audience ask that never get addressed in content?
- Which trending formats or features does the competitor not use?
- Are there adjacent audiences the competitor ignores?

### Threats

Competitor activities that could undermine the target brand:

- **Dominant share of voice**: The competitor dominates conversation in the niche
- **Superior production quality**: Significantly higher-quality content that sets audience expectations
- **Aggressive posting cadence**: Out-publishing the target brand by a wide margin
- **Influencer lock-in**: Exclusive relationships with key industry influencers
- **First-mover advantage**: Already established authority on key topics
- **Budget disparity**: Significantly higher ad spend or production budget

## 3. Competitive Positioning Maps

Plot competitors on two-axis maps to visualize relative positioning and identify whitespace.

### Axis Selection

Choose two axes that are most relevant to the industry and audience. Common combinations:

**Map 1: Price-Personality**

```
                    Premium
                       |
                       |
    Serious -------- + -------- Playful
                       |
                       |
                    Budget
```

**Map 2: Content-Community**

```
                  Educational
                       |
                       |
    Brand-centric -- + -- Community-centric
                       |
                       |
                  Entertaining
```

**Map 3: Digital Maturity**

```
                  Innovative
                       |
                       |
    Niche audience - + - Mass audience
                       |
                       |
                  Traditional
```

### Mapping Process

1. Analyze each competitor's social presence across all active platforms.
2. Score each competitor on both axes (1-10 scale).
3. Plot positions on the map.
4. Identify clusters (where multiple competitors occupy similar space).
5. Identify whitespace (under-represented positions).
6. Determine the target brand's current position and desired position.

### Interpreting the Map

- **Crowded quadrants** indicate commodity positioning; differentiation is harder here.
- **Empty quadrants** may represent opportunity or may be empty for good reason (no audience demand).
- **Movement arrows** can show where the target brand wants to migrate its positioning.

## 4. Content Gap Analysis

Systematic identification of content topics, formats, and themes that represent untapped opportunities.

### Process

#### Step 1: Content Audit per Competitor

For each competitor, categorize their last 30-50 posts into:

| Category          | Description                                    |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Educational       | How-tos, tutorials, tips, explainers           |
| Promotional       | Product features, sales, offers                |
| Entertainment     | Humor, memes, trending content                 |
| Inspirational     | Quotes, success stories, motivation            |
| Community         | UGC reposts, polls, Q&A, shoutouts             |
| Behind-the-scenes | Process, team, culture, making-of              |
| News/Commentary   | Industry trends, opinion pieces                |
| Values-driven     | Social causes, sustainability, mission content |

#### Step 2: Format Audit

Track which formats each competitor uses:

- Static image posts
- Carousel / multi-image posts
- Short-form video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
- Long-form video
- Stories
- Live streams
- Text-only posts (LinkedIn, X)
- Infographics
- User-generated content reposts
- Polls and interactive elements

#### Step 3: Gap Identification

Create a matrix with topics on one axis and competitors on the other. Mark frequency (High / Medium / Low / None).

Gaps fall into three types:

1. **Absolute gaps**: Topics or formats no competitor covers. High opportunity if audience demand exists.
2. **Quality gaps**: Topics competitors cover poorly. Opportunity to create definitive content.
3. **Frequency gaps**: Topics covered rarely when audience interest is high. Opportunity to own the topic through consistent coverage.

#### Step 4: Opportunity Scoring

Score each identified gap on:

| Criterion            | Weight | Scale     |
|----------------------|--------|-----------|
| Audience demand      | 30%    | 1-5       |
| Brand relevance      | 25%    | 1-5       |
| Competitive vacancy  | 20%    | 1-5       |
| Production feasibility | 15%  | 1-5       |
| Platform fit         | 10%    | 1-5       |

**Opportunity Score** = Weighted sum. Prioritize gaps scoring above 3.5.

## 5. Engagement Benchmarking

Establish performance baselines to contextualize the target brand's social media performance.

### Metrics to Benchmark

| Metric                    | Definition                                                | Platform   |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Engagement rate           | (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers * 100             | All        |
| Follower growth rate      | New followers / Total followers per period                 | All        |
| Posting frequency         | Posts per week                                             | All        |
| Video view rate           | Views / Followers * 100                                   | IG, TT, YT |
| Save rate                 | Saves / Reach * 100                                       | Instagram  |
| Share rate                | Shares / Reach * 100                                      | All        |
| Comment sentiment ratio   | Positive comments / Total comments                        | All        |
| Story completion rate     | Views on last story / Views on first story                | IG, FB     |
| Click-through rate        | Link clicks / Impressions * 100                           | LinkedIn   |
| Average response time     | Time to reply to comments/DMs                             | All        |

### Industry Benchmarks (General Ranges)

These are approximate ranges; actual benchmarks vary significantly by industry and audience size.

| Metric            | Low          | Average      | High          |
|-------------------|--------------|--------------|---------------|
| IG Engagement     | < 1%         | 1-3%         | > 3%          |
| FB Engagement     | < 0.5%       | 0.5-1%       | > 1%          |
| LinkedIn Eng.     | < 1%         | 1-2%         | > 2%          |
| TikTok Eng.       | < 3%         | 3-8%         | > 8%          |
| Posting freq.     | < 3/week     | 3-5/week     | > 5/week      |
| Follower growth   | < 1%/month   | 1-3%/month   | > 3%/month    |

## 6. Share of Voice Estimation

Approximate how much of the industry conversation each brand commands.

### Measurement Approach

Since precise share of voice requires paid tools, estimate through:

1. **Follower share**: Sum all competitor followers on each platform. Calculate each brand's percentage.
2. **Content volume share**: Count posts per month across competitors. Calculate percentage.
3. **Engagement share**: Sum total engagements across competitors. Calculate percentage.
4. **Mention share**: Search for brand mentions (via WebSearch) and estimate relative volume.
5. **Hashtag share**: For industry-specific hashtags, estimate which brands appear most frequently.

### Share of Voice Summary Template

```
Platform: [Platform]
Period: [Date range]

| Brand         | Followers | Content Volume | Total Engagement | Est. SOV |
|---------------|-----------|----------------|------------------|----------|
| [Brand A]     | [Count]   | [Posts/month]  | [Total]          | [%]      |
| [Brand B]     | [Count]   | [Posts/month]  | [Total]          | [%]      |
| [Brand C]     | [Count]   | [Posts/month]  | [Total]          | [%]      |
| **Target**    | [Count]   | [Posts/month]  | [Total]          | [%]      |

Estimated Share of Voice = (Avg of Follower%, Content%, Engagement%)
```

### Interpreting Share of Voice

- **SOV > Market Share**: The brand is over-investing in visibility relative to its market position. This is healthy for growth brands.
- **SOV < Market Share**: The brand is under-investing. Competitors may be gaining mindshare.
- **SOV roughly equals Market Share**: Stable position, but vulnerable to aggressive competitors.
