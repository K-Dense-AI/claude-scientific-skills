# Brand Analysis Framework

Reference guide for classifying brands using established frameworks. Used during the Brand DNA synthesis step to assign archetypes, map voice dimensions, extract visual identity, and define content pillars.

## 1. The 12 Brand Archetypes (Jungian)

Each archetype represents a fundamental human motivation. Brands that align with a clear archetype create stronger emotional connections with their audience.

### Innocent

- **Core desire**: Safety, happiness, simplicity
- **Brand promise**: Life can be simple and good
- **Traits**: Optimistic, honest, wholesome, trustworthy
- **Voice**: Warm, simple language, positive framing, avoids negativity
- **Visual cues**: Light colors, soft imagery, clean layouts, natural light
- **Examples in market**: Organic food brands, children's products, wellness brands
- **Content themes**: Tips for simpler living, feel-good stories, pure ingredients

### Explorer

- **Core desire**: Freedom, discovery, self-fulfillment
- **Brand promise**: Break free from the mundane
- **Traits**: Adventurous, independent, pioneering, bold
- **Voice**: Inspirational, action-oriented, uses journey metaphors
- **Visual cues**: Outdoor imagery, expansive landscapes, earth tones, rugged textures
- **Examples in market**: Travel brands, outdoor equipment, vehicles
- **Content themes**: Adventure stories, new discoveries, breaking boundaries

### Sage

- **Core desire**: Knowledge, truth, understanding
- **Brand promise**: The truth will set you free
- **Traits**: Knowledgeable, analytical, thoughtful, credible
- **Voice**: Educational, data-driven, measured, uses evidence
- **Visual cues**: Clean typography, infographics, muted professional palettes
- **Examples in market**: News outlets, consulting firms, educational platforms
- **Content themes**: Research findings, how-it-works, expert analysis, thought leadership

### Hero

- **Core desire**: Mastery, achievement, proving worth
- **Brand promise**: Where there's a will, there's a way
- **Traits**: Courageous, determined, strong, disciplined
- **Voice**: Motivational, direct, empowering, challenge-oriented
- **Visual cues**: Bold contrasts, dynamic imagery, strong angles, athletic figures
- **Examples in market**: Sports brands, military/defense, performance tools
- **Content themes**: Overcoming obstacles, training tips, success stories, challenges

### Outlaw

- **Core desire**: Revolution, liberation, breaking rules
- **Brand promise**: Rules are made to be broken
- **Traits**: Rebellious, disruptive, provocative, radical
- **Voice**: Edgy, confrontational, irreverent, counter-cultural
- **Visual cues**: Dark palettes, gritty textures, unconventional layouts, bold contrasts
- **Examples in market**: Motorcycle brands, alternative fashion, disruptive tech
- **Content themes**: Industry disruption, contrarian takes, breaking conventions

### Magician

- **Core desire**: Transformation, making dreams real
- **Brand promise**: Make the impossible possible
- **Traits**: Visionary, charismatic, imaginative, transformative
- **Voice**: Inspirational, mysterious, uses transformation language
- **Visual cues**: Rich colors, magical/fantastical imagery, gradient effects, depth
- **Examples in market**: Entertainment brands, transformative tech, beauty/cosmetics
- **Content themes**: Before/after transformations, innovation reveals, vision of the future

### Regular Guy/Gal (Everyman)

- **Core desire**: Belonging, connection, fitting in
- **Brand promise**: Everyone matters, everyone belongs
- **Traits**: Down-to-earth, relatable, genuine, unpretentious
- **Voice**: Conversational, friendly, inclusive, uses everyday language
- **Visual cues**: Warm colors, real people (not models), casual settings, approachable design
- **Examples in market**: Family restaurants, home improvement, everyday retail
- **Content themes**: Community stories, everyday tips, relatable humor, user-generated content

### Lover

- **Core desire**: Intimacy, connection, sensory experience
- **Brand promise**: You are worthy of love and desire
- **Traits**: Passionate, sensual, warm, committed, appreciative
- **Voice**: Emotive, sensory language, intimate tone, appreciation-focused
- **Visual cues**: Rich warm tones, luxurious textures, close-up photography, elegant typography
- **Examples in market**: Luxury fashion, fine dining, premium fragrances, jewelry
- **Content themes**: Sensory experiences, beauty appreciation, relationship stories, indulgence

### Jester

- **Core desire**: Joy, fun, living in the moment
- **Brand promise**: Life is too short to be boring
- **Traits**: Playful, humorous, irreverent, spontaneous
- **Voice**: Witty, informal, uses puns and wordplay, self-deprecating
- **Visual cues**: Bright saturated colors, cartoon elements, playful fonts, unexpected layouts
- **Examples in market**: Snack brands, entertainment, casual dining, novelty products
- **Content themes**: Memes, humor, interactive polls, lighthearted takes on trends

### Caregiver

- **Core desire**: Protecting and caring for others
- **Brand promise**: We will take care of you
- **Traits**: Nurturing, compassionate, generous, supportive
- **Voice**: Warm, reassuring, empathetic, service-oriented
- **Visual cues**: Soft colors, human-centric imagery, warm lighting, rounded shapes
- **Examples in market**: Healthcare, insurance, nonprofits, childcare
- **Content themes**: Support resources, community care, patient stories, wellness tips

### Creator

- **Core desire**: Innovation, self-expression, building something lasting
- **Brand promise**: If you can imagine it, it can be made
- **Traits**: Inventive, artistic, perfectionistic, expressive
- **Voice**: Inspired, detail-oriented, craft-focused, appreciative of process
- **Visual cues**: Artistic layouts, craft textures, process imagery, creative palettes
- **Examples in market**: Design tools, art supplies, architecture firms, artisan brands
- **Content themes**: Behind-the-scenes creation, design process, craftsmanship, portfolio showcases

### Ruler

- **Core desire**: Control, order, success, stability
- **Brand promise**: Power and prestige, done right
- **Traits**: Authoritative, refined, responsible, commanding
- **Voice**: Formal, confident, polished, uses prestige language
- **Visual cues**: Dark sophisticated palettes (navy, black, gold), structured layouts, premium materials
- **Examples in market**: Luxury automotive, financial services, high-end hospitality
- **Content themes**: Industry leadership, premium quality, exclusive access, legacy stories

## 2. Voice Dimension Mapping

Map the brand voice on four independent axes. Each axis is a spectrum, not binary. Assign a value from 1-10 where 1 is the left extreme and 10 is the right extreme.

### Axis 1: Formal (1) <---> Casual (10)

| Score | Description                                  | Signals                                   |
|-------|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1-2   | Highly formal                                | Third person, no contractions, long sentences |
| 3-4   | Professional                                 | Some contractions, measured tone           |
| 5-6   | Semiformal                                   | Second person ("you"), accessible language |
| 7-8   | Conversational                               | Colloquialisms, short sentences, fragments |
| 9-10  | Very casual                                  | Slang, emojis, internet shorthand          |

### Axis 2: Serious (1) <---> Playful (10)

| Score | Description                                  | Signals                                   |
|-------|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1-2   | Very serious                                 | No humor, gravity in every statement       |
| 3-4   | Earnest                                      | Occasional lightness, mostly serious       |
| 5-6   | Balanced                                     | Serious when needed, light when appropriate|
| 7-8   | Lighthearted                                 | Frequent humor, upbeat framing             |
| 9-10  | Very playful                                 | Constant wit, puns, memes                  |

### Axis 3: Technical (1) <---> Simple (10)

| Score | Description                                  | Signals                                   |
|-------|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1-2   | Highly technical                             | Domain jargon, assumes expertise           |
| 3-4   | Expert-level                                 | Technical terms with some context          |
| 5-6   | Mixed                                        | Technical when needed, plain otherwise     |
| 7-8   | Accessible                                   | Analogies, everyday language, explains terms|
| 9-10  | Very simple                                  | No jargon, short words, basic vocabulary   |

### Axis 4: Authoritative (1) <---> Friendly (10)

| Score | Description                                  | Signals                                   |
|-------|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1-2   | Very authoritative                           | Commands, declarative statements, top-down |
| 3-4   | Expert                                       | Confident claims, backed by evidence       |
| 5-6   | Guide                                        | Advisory tone, "we recommend"              |
| 7-8   | Peer                                         | Collaborative language, "let's", inclusive  |
| 9-10  | Very friendly                                | Warm, personal, emotionally open           |

## 3. Visual Identity Extraction Methodology

### Color Extraction Process

1. **Primary colors (2-3)**: The dominant brand colors used in logos, headers, CTAs, and navigation. These appear most frequently and carry the strongest brand association.

2. **Secondary colors (2-4)**: Supporting colors used for backgrounds, borders, and secondary UI elements. They complement the primary palette without competing.

3. **Accent colors (1-2)**: High-contrast colors used sparingly for emphasis -- alerts, badges, hover states, or promotional elements.

#### Where to Look

| Element               | Likely Yields              |
|-----------------------|----------------------------|
| Logo                  | Primary colors             |
| Navigation bar        | Primary or secondary       |
| CTA buttons           | Primary or accent          |
| Page backgrounds      | Secondary                  |
| Footer                | Secondary or primary dark  |
| Hover states          | Accent                     |
| Alert/success badges  | Accent                     |

#### CSS Extraction Points

- `:root` or `html` custom properties (`--color-primary`, `--brand-*`)
- `header`, `nav`, `.hero` background and color declarations
- `button`, `.btn`, `.cta` background-color
- `body` and `main` background-color
- `a` and `a:hover` color values
- Tailwind config if present (`tailwind.config.js` theme colors)

### Typography Extraction Process

1. **Heading font**: The typeface used for `h1`-`h3` elements. Often a display or serif font.
2. **Body font**: The typeface used for `p`, `li`, and general text. Often a sans-serif for readability.
3. **Accent font**: Optional. Used for quotes, callouts, or decorative elements.

#### Where to Look

- `<link>` tags pointing to Google Fonts or Adobe Fonts
- `@font-face` declarations in CSS
- `font-family` on `body`, `h1`, `.heading` selectors
- Tailwind `fontFamily` configuration

### Photography Style Classification

Analyze hero images, about page photos, blog thumbnails, and product imagery. Classify into:

| Style              | Description                                           |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| Lifestyle          | People using the product in real-world settings        |
| Product-focused    | Clean product shots, often on white/minimal backgrounds|
| Abstract           | Patterns, textures, or conceptual imagery              |
| People-centric     | Portraits, team photos, candid human moments           |
| Illustration       | Custom illustrations, icons, vector art                |
| Data visualization | Charts, graphs, dashboards                             |
| Stock minimal      | Generic, clean stock photography                       |
| UGC-style          | User-generated, authentic, slightly rough              |

## 4. Content Pillar Definition Framework

Content pillars are 3-5 recurring themes that anchor all brand content. They should be:

- **Distinct**: Each pillar covers a clearly different topic area.
- **Audience-aligned**: Each pillar addresses a specific audience need or interest.
- **Brand-authentic**: Each pillar connects to the brand's values, expertise, or mission.
- **Platform-flexible**: Each pillar can produce content across all target platforms.
- **Sustainable**: Each pillar has enough depth for months of content.

### Pillar Discovery Process

1. **From values**: Map each core value to a content theme. "Innovation" becomes an "Industry Insights" pillar.
2. **From audience pain points**: Each major pain point can anchor a pillar. "Time management struggles" becomes a "Productivity Tips" pillar.
3. **From competitive whitespace**: Topics competitors neglect can become differentiating pillars.
4. **From existing high-performing content**: If the brand already publishes, identify what resonates most.
5. **From industry conversations**: What topics generate the most discussion in the industry?

### Pillar Structure Template

For each pillar, define:

```
Pillar Name: [Short, memorable name]
Description: [1-2 sentences explaining the theme]
Audience Need: [What need this addresses]
Content Formats: [Blog, video, carousel, story, etc.]
Example Topics:
  - [Specific topic 1]
  - [Specific topic 2]
  - [Specific topic 3]
Posting Frequency: [X times per week/month]
KPIs: [Engagement rate, saves, shares, etc.]
```

### Common Pillar Categories

| Category        | Works Well For                | Example Content                     |
|-----------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Educational     | Sage, Creator, Caregiver      | How-tos, tutorials, explainers      |
| Inspirational   | Hero, Explorer, Magician      | Success stories, motivational posts  |
| Behind-the-scenes | Creator, Regular Guy, Lover | Process reveals, team spotlights     |
| Community       | Caregiver, Regular Guy        | User features, Q&A, polls           |
| Product/Service | All archetypes                | Features, use cases, testimonials    |
| Industry news   | Sage, Ruler                   | Trend analysis, commentary           |
| Entertainment   | Jester, Outlaw                | Memes, humor, trending audio         |
| Values-driven   | Caregiver, Innocent, Hero     | Cause content, impact stories        |
