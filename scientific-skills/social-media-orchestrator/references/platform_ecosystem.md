Decision matrix and strategic guide for selecting and optimizing social media platforms. Use this reference to match platforms to brand objectives, audience demographics, and content capabilities.

## Instagram

### Overview
The dominant visual platform for brand building, product discovery, and community engagement. Instagram rewards aesthetic consistency, creative Reels, and genuine interaction.

### Strengths
- Largest engaged visual audience
- Multiple content formats (Feed, Stories, Reels, Live, Guides)
- Strong shopping and product discovery features
- Carousel posts drive high saves and shares
- Reels algorithm provides significant organic reach boost
- Robust creator economy and influencer partnerships

### Weaknesses
- Organic reach for feed posts has declined significantly
- Algorithm changes frequently, requiring constant adaptation
- Saturated with brands; standing out requires high-quality content
- Link sharing limited (bio link, Stories link sticker)
- Engagement rates trending downward industry-wide

### Best Content Types (ordered by algorithm preference)
1. **Reels** (15-90 seconds): Highest organic reach, trending audio boosts
2. **Carousels** (up to 20 slides): Highest save rate, educational content
3. **Stories**: Daily touchpoint, polls/quizzes for engagement
4. **Single image posts**: Brand aesthetic anchors
5. **Live**: Real-time engagement, Q&A sessions
6. **Guides**: Curated collections, evergreen reference

### Algorithm Priorities
- **Reels**: Watch time completion, shares via DMs, saves, original audio usage
- **Feed**: Saves > Shares > Comments > Likes (in order of signal weight)
- **Stories**: Replies, sticker interactions, watch-through rate
- **General**: Posting consistency, niche relevance, early engagement velocity

### Growth Tactics
1. Post Reels 4-7x per week using trending audio
2. Use 20-30 hashtags (mix of broad, niche, and branded)
3. Engage with 50+ accounts in your niche daily (comments, not just likes)
4. Carousel posts for educational content (aim for saves)
5. Collaborate with micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) for authentic reach
6. Use Stories daily with interactive stickers
7. Cross-post Reels to TikTok and YouTube Shorts

### When to Prioritize Instagram
- Visual product or lifestyle brand
- Target audience is 18-44
- Content is inherently visual (food, fashion, travel, design, fitness)
- Building a brand aesthetic is important
- E-commerce or product-based business

### Posting Cadence
| Format | Recommended Frequency |
|--------|----------------------|
| Reels | 4-7x per week |
| Feed posts | 3-5x per week |
| Stories | Daily (3-7 frames) |
| Carousels | 2-3x per week |
| Live | 1-2x per month |

### Optimal Posting Times (General)
- Weekdays: 7-9 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-7 PM (local time)
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Stories: Mornings and evenings when users browse casually
- Note: Always validate with account-specific analytics

## LinkedIn

### Overview
The premier B2B platform for professional networking, thought leadership, and lead generation. LinkedIn uniquely rewards personal, authentic content from individuals over polished brand content.

### Strengths
- Highest quality B2B audience and lead generation
- Organic reach is currently very high (compared to other platforms)
- Text-only posts perform exceptionally well
- Document/carousel posts have high engagement
- Newsletter feature builds subscriber base
- Professional context means high-intent audience

### Weaknesses
- Limited B2C applications
- Content can feel dry without effort to humanize
- Video adoption is slower than other platforms
- Smaller total audience vs. Instagram/TikTok
- Algorithm can feel unpredictable
- Limited visual creativity compared to Instagram

### Best Content Types (ordered by algorithm preference)
1. **Text posts** (with line breaks, hooks, storytelling): Highest organic reach
2. **Document/PDF carousels**: High engagement and saves
3. **Polls**: Algorithm-boosted, conversation starters
4. **Native video**: Growing in importance
5. **Articles/Newsletters**: Long-form thought leadership
6. **Image posts**: Support text messaging visually
7. **Reposts with commentary**: Adds to conversation

### Algorithm Priorities
- **Dwell time**: How long people spend reading your post
- **Early engagement**: Comments in first 60-90 minutes are critical
- **Meaningful comments**: Longer comments signal higher quality
- **Network relevance**: Content shown to connections first, then broader
- **Content type variety**: Rewarded for mixing formats
- **Post frequency**: 1x/day sweet spot; more can dilute

### Growth Tactics
1. Write hooks that stop the scroll (first 2 lines visible before "see more")
2. Personal stories outperform corporate messaging consistently
3. Comment on 20+ relevant posts daily to increase visibility
4. Post document carousels for educational content (swipeable PDFs)
5. Use 3-5 relevant hashtags (fewer than Instagram)
6. Tag relevant people sparingly (only when genuinely relevant)
7. Engage with every comment on your posts within 1 hour
8. Share failures and lessons, not just wins

### When to Prioritize LinkedIn
- B2B product or service
- Target audience is professionals, decision-makers, or enterprises
- Thought leadership is a core brand strategy
- Lead generation for high-ticket products/services
- Recruiting or employer branding
- Professional services (consulting, SaaS, finance, legal)

### Posting Cadence
| Format | Recommended Frequency |
|--------|----------------------|
| Text posts | 3-5x per week |
| Document carousels | 1-2x per week |
| Polls | 1x per week |
| Video | 1-2x per week |
| Articles | 1-2x per month |
| Newsletter | Weekly or biweekly |

### Optimal Posting Times (General)
- Weekdays: 7-8 AM, 12 PM, 5-6 PM (local time)
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Avoid weekends (significantly lower engagement)
- Note: B2B audiences are most active during business hours

## TikTok

### Overview
The fastest-growing platform with unmatched organic reach potential. TikTok's algorithm is uniquely merit-based, meaning content quality matters more than follower count. Entertainment-first platform where brands must adapt to the culture, not the other way around.

### Strengths
- Highest organic reach of any major platform
- Algorithm serves content to relevant audiences regardless of follower count
- Trend participation provides massive reach boosts
- Authentic, unpolished content often outperforms produced content
- Gen Z and Millennial audience highly engaged
- Duets and Stitches enable collaborative content
- Shopping features expanding rapidly

### Weaknesses
- Content requires high volume and frequent posting
- Trends move extremely fast (24-72 hour lifecycle)
- Audience skews younger; not ideal for all demographics
- Content style may not suit all brands (especially formal B2B)
- Analytics are less robust than Instagram/Facebook
- Older demographic adoption still growing

### Best Content Types (ordered by algorithm preference)
1. **Trend-format videos**: Using trending sounds and formats
2. **Educational/how-to**: "Did you know" style, quick tips
3. **Behind-the-scenes**: Authentic, unfiltered brand content
4. **Storytelling**: Hook-driven narratives
5. **Duets/Stitches**: Responding to or building on other content
6. **Product demos**: Show, don't tell
7. **Day-in-the-life**: Relatable, human content

### Algorithm Priorities
- **Watch time**: Single most important signal (percentage completed)
- **Rewatch rate**: Loops and replays are very strong signals
- **Shares**: Shares via DMs are weighted heavily
- **Comments**: Volume and sentiment
- **Following from video**: Strongest intent signal
- **Video information**: Captions, sounds, hashtags for topic matching

### Growth Tactics
1. Post 1-3x daily (volume is rewarded)
2. Hook viewers in first 1-3 seconds (critical)
3. Use trending sounds within 24-48 hours of emergence
4. Keep videos 15-60 seconds for optimal completion rates
5. Use text overlays for accessibility and engagement
6. End with a CTA or question to drive comments
7. Cross-post to Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts
8. Participate in challenges and trends authentically

### When to Prioritize TikTok
- Target audience includes Gen Z or younger Millennials (16-35)
- Brand can be entertaining, authentic, and trend-adaptive
- Product is visually demonstrable
- Building organic reach is a priority over direct conversion
- Content creation capacity supports high posting volume
- Brand is comfortable with imperfect, authentic content

### Posting Cadence
| Format | Recommended Frequency |
|--------|----------------------|
| Short videos (15-60s) | 1-3x per day |
| Longer videos (1-3 min) | 2-3x per week |
| Live | 1-2x per week |
| Stories | Daily |

### Optimal Posting Times (General)
- Weekdays: 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-11 PM (local time)
- Best days: Tuesday, Thursday, Friday
- Weekend evenings can perform well
- Note: TikTok audience is active later in the day than LinkedIn

## Facebook

### Overview
The largest social network with the broadest demographic reach. While organic reach has declined significantly, Facebook remains powerful for community building through Groups, local business promotion, and paid advertising with sophisticated targeting.

### Strengths
- Largest user base globally
- Facebook Groups are powerful community tools
- Most sophisticated advertising platform (Meta Ads)
- Strong for local businesses and events
- Marketplace for product sales
- Cross-posting with Instagram streamlines content

### Weaknesses
- Organic reach is very low for brand Pages (1-5% typical)
- Younger demographics are less active
- Content must compete with friends and family posts
- Algorithm heavily favors paid content
- Engagement rates are generally lower than Instagram/TikTok

### Best Content Types (ordered by algorithm preference)
1. **Video** (especially live): Highest organic reach
2. **Group posts**: Community-driven engagement
3. **Reels**: Growing in priority (mirroring Instagram)
4. **Events**: Local and virtual event promotion
5. **Link posts**: Sharing articles and resources
6. **Photo albums**: Multi-image storytelling
7. **Text posts**: Can work in Groups and personal profiles

### Algorithm Priorities
- **Meaningful interactions**: Comments, shares, and reactions from friends
- **Groups content**: Prioritized in news feed
- **Video watch time**: Longer views signal quality
- **Shares to Messenger**: Private shares are strong signals
- **Post type diversity**: Mix of content formats

### Growth Tactics
1. Build and nurture a Facebook Group around your brand's niche
2. Use Facebook Live for real-time engagement (algorithm boost)
3. Create and promote Facebook Events
4. Invest in Meta Ads for Page growth and conversions
5. Cross-post Instagram content to Facebook
6. Engage in relevant Groups (not your own) to build authority
7. Use Facebook Marketplace for product-based businesses

### When to Prioritize Facebook
- Broad demographic target (25-65)
- Local or community-focused business
- Running paid social campaigns (Meta Ads)
- Building community through Groups
- Event-based marketing
- E-commerce with Facebook/Instagram Shops

### Posting Cadence
| Format | Recommended Frequency |
|--------|----------------------|
| Feed posts | 3-5x per week |
| Stories/Reels | Daily |
| Group posts | 3-5x per week |
| Live | 1-2x per month |
| Events | As needed |

### Optimal Posting Times (General)
- Weekdays: 9-11 AM, 1-3 PM (local time)
- Best days: Wednesday, Thursday, Friday
- Weekends: Lower but can work for B2C
- Note: Facebook audience is most active midday

## Twitter/X

### Overview
The real-time conversation platform. Twitter/X is best for news, opinions, industry commentary, and B2B networking. It rewards quick wit, hot takes, and genuine expertise. Threads allow for deeper storytelling.

### Strengths
- Real-time conversation and trend participation
- Strong B2B and tech community
- Threads allow long-form storytelling
- Direct engagement with industry leaders
- Viral potential through retweets and quote tweets
- News and opinion leadership

### Weaknesses
- High noise level; content has short shelf life
- Rapid content churn requires high posting volume
- Platform stability and feature changes create uncertainty
- Limited visual storytelling compared to Instagram
- Smaller active user base than other platforms
- Engagement can skew toward negativity

### Best Content Types (ordered by algorithm preference)
1. **Threads**: Multi-tweet storytelling, tutorials, insights
2. **Engagement tweets**: Questions, polls, hot takes
3. **Image tweets**: Screenshots, infographics, data visualizations
4. **Quote tweets**: Adding commentary to trending content
5. **Video**: Short clips, commentary
6. **Spaces**: Live audio discussions

### Algorithm Priorities
- **Replies and conversations**: Two-way engagement weighted heavily
- **Retweets with commentary**: Higher signal than plain retweets
- **Likes**: Base engagement signal
- **Recency**: Content shelf life is short (2-6 hours)
- **Topic relevance**: Content shown to users interested in your topics

### Growth Tactics
1. Tweet 3-5x daily minimum
2. Write threads (5-15 tweets) for in-depth content
3. Engage in relevant conversations by quoting and replying
4. Use 1-3 hashtags per tweet (more seems spammy)
5. Post data and insights with visual charts
6. Host or participate in Twitter/X Spaces
7. Be opinionated and authentic (bland content underperforms)

### When to Prioritize Twitter/X
- B2B tech or SaaS brand
- Real-time communication is important (news, events)
- Brand voice is opinionated, witty, or expert
- Target audience is in tech, media, finance, or politics
- Thought leadership is a primary content strategy
- Need for rapid customer communication

### Posting Cadence
| Format | Recommended Frequency |
|--------|----------------------|
| Tweets | 3-5x per day |
| Threads | 2-3x per week |
| Polls | 1-2x per week |
| Spaces | 1-2x per month |

### Optimal Posting Times (General)
- Weekdays: 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM (local time)
- Best days: Monday through Thursday
- Tweets during breaking news or trending topics get amplified
- Note: Short content shelf life means timing matters more here

## Platform Selection Framework

### Step 1: Audience Match
Identify where your target audience spends time:
- Gen Z (16-24): TikTok (primary), Instagram (secondary)
- Millennials (25-40): Instagram (primary), TikTok, LinkedIn
- Gen X (41-56): Facebook (primary), LinkedIn, Instagram
- Boomers (57-75): Facebook (primary)
- Professionals/B2B: LinkedIn (primary), Twitter/X (secondary)

### Step 2: Content Capability Match
Match your content production capacity to platform demands:
- **High video production**: TikTok, Instagram Reels
- **Strong photography**: Instagram, Facebook
- **Strong writing**: LinkedIn, Twitter/X
- **Community management capacity**: Facebook Groups, all platforms
- **Limited resources**: Pick 1-2 platforms and do them well

### Step 3: Business Model Match
- **E-commerce/DTC**: Instagram + TikTok
- **B2B SaaS**: LinkedIn + Twitter/X
- **Local business**: Facebook + Instagram
- **Personal brand**: LinkedIn + Twitter/X + Instagram
- **Media/Entertainment**: TikTok + Instagram + Twitter/X
- **Professional services**: LinkedIn + Instagram

### Step 4: Resource Allocation
For most brands, the recommended approach:
- **1 Primary platform**: 50% of effort, where core audience lives
- **1 Secondary platform**: 30% of effort, growth opportunity
- **1 Experimental platform**: 20% of effort, testing new audiences

Do not spread across all platforms equally. Depth on fewer platforms beats shallow presence on many.
