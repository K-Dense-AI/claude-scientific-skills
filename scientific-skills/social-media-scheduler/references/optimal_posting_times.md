# Optimal Posting Times Reference

Data synthesized from Sprout Social (2024-2025), Hootsuite (2024-2025), Buffer (2024), and Later (2024) research reports covering millions of posts across industries.

All times are listed in the poster's local timezone unless noted otherwise.

---

## Instagram

### Best Days (Ranked)

1. **Tuesday** -- highest overall engagement
2. **Wednesday** -- strong mid-week performance
3. **Monday** -- business audiences active early in the week
4. **Thursday** -- sustained mid-week engagement
5. **Friday** -- engagement drops after 2 PM
6. **Saturday** -- moderate, peaks late morning
7. **Sunday** -- lowest engagement overall

### Best Hours

| Rank | Time Window | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| 1 | 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM | Peak engagement across all segments |
| 2 | 7:00 AM - 8:00 AM | Early commute scrolling |
| 3 | 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM | Lunch break engagement |
| 4 | 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM | Post-work browsing |
| 5 | 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM | Evening leisure (Reels perform well) |

### Worst Times

- Before 6:00 AM
- 3:00 PM - 4:00 PM (afternoon slump)
- After 10:00 PM on weekdays

### Frequency Recommendations

- **Minimum**: 3 posts/week (feed) + 5 stories/week
- **Optimal**: 5-7 posts/week (feed) + daily stories
- **Maximum**: 2 feed posts/day, 10 stories/day
- **Reels**: 3-5 per week for growth

## Weekend vs Weekday Patterns

### Weekdays (Monday - Friday)

- **LinkedIn**: 85-100% of engagement occurs on weekdays. Weekends are negligible.
- **Instagram**: Weekdays account for approximately 70% of engagement. Tuesday-Thursday form the core.
- **Facebook**: Weekday engagement is 65-75% of total. Wednesday is the standout day.
- **TikTok**: Weekday vs weekend split is roughly 60/40. More balanced than other platforms.

### Weekends (Saturday - Sunday)

- **Instagram**: Saturday morning (9:00-11:00 AM) sees a spike for lifestyle, travel, and food content. Sunday is the weakest day across most verticals.
- **TikTok**: Saturday is viable for entertainment, lifestyle, and challenge content. Sunday shows moderate engagement.
- **Facebook**: Weekend engagement is lower but consistent for community-oriented pages. Event content performs better on weekends.
- **LinkedIn**: Avoid weekends entirely for organic content. Sponsored content can still perform as feeds are less competitive.

## Holiday and Event Considerations

### Engagement Shifts During Holidays

- **Major holidays** (Christmas, Thanksgiving, New Year): engagement drops 30-50% on all platforms. Schedule lighter, celebratory content or pause.
- **3-day weekends** (Memorial Day, Labor Day, etc.): Friday engagement drops; Saturday and Sunday may see slight increases for leisure content.
- **Back-to-school** (August-September): B2C and e-commerce see engagement spikes, especially on Instagram and TikTok.
- **Black Friday / Cyber Monday**: Instagram and Facebook engagement for e-commerce peaks; LinkedIn engagement drops.
- **New Year / January**: "New year, new me" content peaks on Instagram and TikTok (fitness, productivity, self-improvement).
- **Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Father's Day**: Gift and lifestyle content spikes on Instagram and Facebook.

### Event-Based Adjustments

- **Industry conferences**: Post during and immediately after sessions for LinkedIn engagement spikes.
- **Product launches**: Increase frequency 2-3x for the launch week across all platforms.
- **Cultural moments** (awards shows, sports events): Real-time or same-day content on TikTok and Instagram can capture trending engagement.
- **Platform-specific events** (Instagram's anniversary, TikTok trends): Align content to take advantage of algorithm boosts.

### General Holiday Rules

1. Reduce posting volume by 30-50% during major holidays.
2. Schedule evergreen or celebratory content instead of promotional posts.
3. Increase volume in the 1-2 weeks before shopping holidays.
4. Monitor real-time engagement and adjust if a holiday unexpectedly boosts traffic.
5. Always localize holidays to the target audience's geography.
